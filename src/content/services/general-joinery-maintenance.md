h1: General aluminium joinery maintenance
lead: Servicing tired aluminium windows and doors so they last another decade. Seals, alignment, drainage, hardware, the lot, all sorted in a single visit. Residential and commercial across Christchurch and Canterbury.

fixes:
  - Worn or perished weather seals letting in draughts and water
  - Tracks clogged with dirt, rollers running rough
  - Hardware that's drifted out of alignment over time
  - Blocked weep holes causing water to pool
  - Loose catches, latches, and keepers
  - Sashes and doors that have dropped or twisted slightly
  - Hardware fixings that have worked loose

signs:
  - Joinery is more than 5 years old and hasn't been serviced
  - You're noticing small operational issues across multiple windows
  - Pre-sale property inspection coming up
  - Coastal location with salt exposure
  - Commercial property due for its annual round
  - Earthquake or settlement movement has shifted things

process:
  - step: Walk-through assessment
    detail: We go through every window and door, noting what needs attention. You get a single quote covering the whole job.
  - step: Seals, hardware, alignment
    detail: Weather seals replaced where perished, tracks cleaned and lubricated, hardware adjusted, catches and keepers re-aligned.
  - step: Drainage and weatherproofing
    detail: Weep holes cleared, drainage paths checked, gaps in seals or beads addressed.
  - step: Function test on every opening
    detail: Each sash and door tested, adjusted, and signed off. Ones that aren't operating like new get fixed before we leave.

pricing: Quoted per property after walk-through. Typical 3-bedroom home is a half-day visit. Commercial maintenance rounds are quoted per visit and per opening, with optional annual scheduling.

faqs:
  - q: Is this worth it if my windows are working OK?
    a: Yes. Most aluminium joinery failures happen gradually — a seal goes a bit soft, a roller wears, a catch loosens. A maintenance visit catches small issues before they become expensive repairs or full replacements.
  - q: How often should aluminium joinery be serviced?
    a: Every 3 to 5 years on residential. Annually on commercial properties or coastal homes where salt exposure accelerates wear. Most people find a service every few years pays for itself.
  - q: How long does a maintenance visit take?
    a: A typical 3-bedroom home is around half a day. Larger homes or commercial properties are quoted per visit after walk-through.
  - q: Do you bring your own seals and parts?
    a: We carry common sizes in the van for same-day fixes. Specialist or non-standard items are sourced and fitted on a return visit if needed.
  - q: Can you do annual rounds for commercial properties?
    a: Yes. We schedule recurring maintenance for shopfronts, body corporates, schools, and rentals. Keeps small issues from turning into expensive ones.
