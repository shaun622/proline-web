process:
  - step: Diagnose the failure
    detail: We work out whether it's lubrication, alignment, a worn cylinder, or a failed mechanism. That decides whether to repair or replace.
  - step: Repair where possible
    detail: Lubrication, adjustment, cylinder rekey, or mechanism overhaul. Cheaper than replacement where the unit is sound.
  - step: Replace where past it
    detail: Where the lock is worn out or no longer secure, we fit a current equivalent. Brand-matched or upgraded to a higher security spec.
  - step: Test and tag
    detail: Every lock we touch gets tested, keys cut and labelled, and any spare keys handed over before we leave.

    a: For most lock types, yes. Saves the cost of new hardware and you get fresh keys with the same lock. Useful when keys have been lost or after a tenant change.
