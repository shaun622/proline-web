process:
  - step: On-site assessment
    detail: We check your existing frames are suitable. Most aluminium frames in good condition are. We'll be honest if they're not, and recommend full replacement instead.

faqs:
  - q: How much does retrofit double glazing cost?
    a: It varies by window size, glass spec, and how many panes. As a rule it's a fraction of the cost of full new joinery, because the frames stay. We quote per-pane after measure so you get an exact number.
  - q: Is it as good as full new double-glazed joinery?
    a: For thermal performance, yes. The insulating glass unit (the IGU) is what does most of the heat-keeping work. Modern IGUs deliver the same R-values whether the frame is new or existing aluminium. The frame itself isn't where most heat is lost.
  - q: How long does the install take?
    a: Most homes take half a day to a full day, depending on the number of panes. We work room-by-room with minimal disruption.
  - q: Will retrofit fix all my window problems?
    a: It fixes thermal and acoustic issues. If hardware, seals, or alignment also need attention, we sort those at the same time so you get the full benefit.
  - q: Are my existing frames suitable for retrofit?
    a: Most aluminium frames in sound condition are. We confirm during the on-site assessment. Frames with significant corrosion or damaged profiles may be better candidates for full replacement.
